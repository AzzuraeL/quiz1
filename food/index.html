Local Food from Trenggalek

Discover the delicious traditional foods from Trenggalek, known for their unique flavors and cultural significance.

Alen-alen is a traditional snack from Trenggalek, East Java, Indonesia. It is called “alen-alen” because of its round shape with a hole in the middle, resembling “ali-ali” in Javanese, which means ring. Alen-alen is made from cassava, has a savory taste, and a very hard yet crunchy texture, with its distinctive yellow color.

These savory-tasting chips are highly sought after by tourists because they are well-known in many regions. It is no surprise that Trenggalek Regency has earned the nickname “The City of Tempe Chips.” The signature tempe chips from Trenggalek are thin in shape. This thin form originates from the very beginning of the production process, as the tempe itself is traditionally made that way. The tempe chips are processed using a seasoned flour batter mixed with slices of kaffir lime leaves, giving them a delicious flavor and a crispy texture. Over time, local MSME producers of tempe chips have created innovations by adding a bit of flavoring to the chips, resulting in two variations: original and spicy.

Sego Gegok is a traditional dish from Trenggalek, East Java, which can also be written as Gegog. The difference in the final letter comes from the Javanese pronunciation of the consonants K and G, but this does not change the meaning of the name. The word Gegok or Gegog is actually an abbreviation of the Javanese phrase “Geneman Godong Gedhang”, where Geneman means filling or wrapping, Godong means leaf, and Gedhang means banana. Thus, Nasi Gegok refers to rice wrapped in banana leaves. Inside the wrapping, the rice is combined with a variety of side dishes that are both appetizing and flavorful, often cooked with added spices that give the dish a distinctly spicy taste.
