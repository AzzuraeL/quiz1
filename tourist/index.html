Prigi Beach is a southern coastal beach located along the Indian Ocean and administratively part of Tasimadu Village, Watulimo District, Trenggalek Regency, East Java, Indonesia. It is about 48 km from the center of Trenggalek city. In addition to being a popular tourist destination, Prigi Beach also serves as a national port and is the largest fishing harbor on the southern coast of Java. Visitors to this beach can enjoy activities such as fishing, camping, playing tennis, or simply staying in one of the nearby hotels while taking in the beautiful atmosphere. Prigi Beach becomes especially lively during the Javanese month of Selo, when local fishermen hold the traditional Larung Sembonyo ceremony, an annual ritual of gratitude to God for the abundant harvest of the sea.

Goa Lowo (Indonesian: Bat Cave) is one of the natural tourist attractions located in Trenggalek Regency, East Java. It is the longest cave in Southeast Asia, situated in Watulimo District, Trenggalek Regency, with a total length of approximately 2 km. However, only 850 meters can be explored by visitors, as the remaining 1,150 meters are not yet accessible and are cut off by a strong river current. Lowo Cave is located about 27 km from the centers of both Trenggalek and Tulungagung.
